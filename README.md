Using the Open Food Facts database, I analyzed the origins of avocados, lemons, sourdough-related grains, and olive-oil ingredients to understand how global supply chains come together to create a single brunch favorite.

This project involved filtering messy food-category data, joining CSV/TXT files, cleaning multi-tag fields, and extracting country-of-origin information to map where our everyday ingredients really come from.

Amazing how one simple plate can rely on supply chains spanning multiple continents.

In this project, I conducted a supply chain analysis of three core ingredients used in avocado toast using Open Food Facts data. I filtered noisy category tags, joined ingredient files with their relevant category lists, extracted country-of-origin information, and built cleaned datasets showing where each ingredient is produced.

The work highlights how global, diverse, and interconnected the supply chain is for even the simplest modern dishes ‚Äî and sets the foundation for further analysis on transportation distances and time-at-sea.
